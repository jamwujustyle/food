Migration, Memory, and the Language of Home Cooking

A university research project exploring how culinary traditions serve as bridges between past and present, homeland and new home, preserving cultural identity through the universal language of food.

Cultural Heritage Identity & Belonging Culinary Traditions

About This Project

Our project explores how migration shapes people's relationships with food, language, and cultural identity. When individuals or families move across borders, they carry their recipes, food memories, and cooking traditions with them. These traditions become a way to remember home and express belonging in new environments. Through our research, we examine how food acts as a bridge between past and present, and how the language of cooking—recipes, storytelling, and shared meals—preserves memory and identity.

We analyze academic perspectives on foodways, cultural memory, and globalization to understand how home cooking reflects both personal history and collective identity. By combining cultural theory with real examples of migrant food practices, our project highlights the emotional and linguistic power of food in shaping who we are. Ultimately, "Migration, Memory, and the Language of Home Cooking" celebrates food as a universal form of communication that connects people across time, place, and culture.
