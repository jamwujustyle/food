Autobiography

I’m Zhamshid Abdulazizov, a student who grew up in Kazakhstan, where meals were as much about conversation and family rituals as about eating. I remember the long weekends when my family gathered around beshbarmak or baursaks, debating flavors, sharing stories, and sometimes playfully arguing over who added too much salt. From those moments, I realized that food is a lens for understanding people—what they value, how they remember, and how they connect. Cooking became my way to explore these dynamics personally: experimenting with traditional Kazakh dishes, introducing subtle twists, and seeing how friends respond. This curiosity led me to notice how recipes, ingredients, and preparation styles carry identity, memory, and social meaning.

Reflection

1. Key Learnings

One key lesson I took from this course is that food is a map of culture, identity, and power. I learned that globalization and migration don’t just spread dishes—they reshape them. A dish from Kazakhstan, for example, can appear differently abroad, acquiring new meanings while still carrying a trace of its origin. I also noticed how language frames food: the way people name dishes, describe flavors, or tell cooking stories preserves identity and transmits memory across generations. Understanding these patterns revealed that even everyday meals are embedded in larger social and cultural systems.

2. Personal Motto

“Every dish carries a story.” This reminds me that cooking is more than technique; it’s communication. A family recipe, a street snack, or a holiday feast reflects history, relationships, and personal choices. Paying attention to these stories has deepened my appreciation for both my own roots and other cultures.

3. Applying Course Themes

I plan to apply these insights by observing how people use food to express identity and memory. At home, I’ll pay attention to how recipes evolve across generations; in my academic work, I’ll explore how dishes travel and adapt while retaining cultural significance. Food, for me, will remain a way to connect with others, uncover hidden histories, and understand culture in its lived, flavorful form.

"Food is memory mode tangible, tradition mode taste-able."
